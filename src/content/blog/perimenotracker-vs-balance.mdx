# Peri Tracker vs Balance: Which App Is Better for Perimenopause?

Balance is the most-downloaded menopause app in the UK and has significant name recognition. Peri Tracker is built specifically for the perimenopause phase with a focus on daily logging and clinical utility. They are not direct substitutes — they are built around different primary goals — which is why the "which is better" question depends entirely on what you need from an app.

This comparison is direct. We make Peri Tracker, so you should weigh that context. What we can offer is a clear account of where each app genuinely excels and where it falls short.

## What Balance Does Well

Balance was developed with Dr. Louise Newson, a menopause specialist, and the educational content reflects that collaboration. The articles, guides, and expert Q&As are substantive and accurate. For women who are newly experiencing perimenopause symptoms and want to understand what's happening — the hormonal biology, what treatments exist, what to expect — Balance is one of the better resources available in app form.

The community aspect is also genuine. Balance has a large, active user base, and the community features give women a space to share experiences, ask questions, and hear from others at the same stage of life. For many women, this social and educational dimension is exactly what they need.

## Where Balance Falls Short for Symptom Tracking

Balance's symptom tracking is functional but not its core strength. The range of loggable symptoms is narrower than a dedicated tracker, severity ratings are limited, and the data presentation focuses more on general summaries than on the kind of trend analysis useful for medical conversations.

There is no structured PDF export for doctor visits. If your goal is to generate a report you can hand to a gynecologist — cycle history, weekly symptom frequencies, severity trends — Balance's current feature set does not support that directly.

Balance also stores data on external servers, which is a consideration for women who prefer not to share health data with third parties.

## What Peri Tracker Does Well

Peri Tracker is built around one workflow: daily symptom logging that produces clinically useful data. The symptom set covers the full range relevant to perimenopause — hot flashes with time of day and severity, sleep quality, mood, energy, brain fog, cycle data, and customizable additional symptoms. Every major symptom includes a severity rating.

The daily log is designed to take under two minutes. The data is stored locally on your device, not on external servers.

The doctor report feature generates a structured PDF covering your last 1–3 months of data: cycle history, top symptoms by frequency and severity, and trend lines. This is designed to be readable by a clinician in the first 60 seconds of an appointment. It is the feature most users cite as most valuable.

Apple Health integration means sleep stage data and resting heart rate flow in automatically on Apple Watch, adding physiological context to your subjective log without additional manual entry.

## Where Peri Tracker Falls Short

Peri Tracker does not have community features. If peer support and shared experience are important to you, the app does not provide that. The educational content is present but lighter than Balance's — the focus is on the tracking tool, not on being a knowledge resource.

The app is iOS-only. Android users cannot use it currently.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose Peri Tracker if** your main goal is to track symptoms consistently and bring useful data to your doctor. The structured daily log, severity ratings, and doctor report are built specifically for this workflow. The local storage is important if you are concerned about health data privacy.

**Choose Balance if** you want to learn about perimenopause, read expert content, and connect with a community. It is also the better choice if you are on Android.

**Use both if** you want the community and education that Balance provides and the clinical tracking utility of Peri Tracker. There is no meaningful overlap in core functionality — they complement each other well.
